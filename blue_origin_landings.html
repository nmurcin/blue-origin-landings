BLUE ORIGIN LANDINGS — a free New Glenn & Blue Moon landing game

BLUE ORIGIN LANDINGS is a free, browser-based rocket landing game inspired by Blue Origin's reusable hardware. Fly the full landing profile — the boostback burn, a fin-and-strake descent, and one nerve-wracking late landing burn — and bring the booster home, then take Blue Moon all the way to the lunar surface. No download, no account, and it plays on your phone. Scroll up to play; read on for how it works.

▶  Play the game

How to play

  • Desktop: steer with the ← → arrow keys, fire the engine with Space (or ↑), and press X for 2× fast-forward.
  • Mobile: hold the BURN button and drag the steering slider to fly.
  • The goal: during the boostback burn, drag your predicted-impact marker onto the target, cut the engine and coast the arc, ride the fins and strakes down, then burn as late as the STOP marker allows. Fuel left over is your score, so the optimal landing is the scariest one.

Three missions

  • New Glenn 7×2 → Jacklyn. The seven-engine first stage flies an upright landing on the sea-based landing platform Jacklyn, hundreds of kilometres downrange.
  • New Glenn 9×4 → Jacklyn. The heavier nine-engine variant comes in faster and steeper — you have to lean on the strakes' cross-range glide to reach the deck, and the touchdown window is tighter.
  • Blue Moon MK1 → the Moon. An uncrewed cargo lander makes a pure propulsive descent in one-sixth gravity with no air — all engine, no flip.

How real rocket landings work

BLUE ORIGIN LANDINGS is built on the real ideas behind reusable rockets — boostback burns, aerodynamic control surfaces, the propulsive "hoverslam", and trans-lunar trajectories. New Glenn uses four actuated aerodynamic control surfaces plus wing-like strakes that add lift and cross-range during the booster's descent, which is the core of the fin-and-strake flight model here. The lunar legs use textbook two-body orbital mechanics. For authoritative information on the real vehicles, see Blue Origin's public pages on New Glenn and Blue Moon.

FAQ

Is BLUE ORIGIN LANDINGS free?
Yes — completely free, with no account needed to play. Joining the local leaderboard just asks for a callsign.
Do I need to download anything to play?
No. It runs entirely in any modern web browser on desktop and mobile — nothing to install.
How do I land the rocket?
Fire the boostback burn to aim your predicted impact point at the target, cut and coast, ride the fins and strakes down, then light one late landing burn timed with the STOP marker. Fuel left over is your score.
Does it work on a phone?
Yes — a one-button cockpit with drag-slider steering, right in the mobile browser.
