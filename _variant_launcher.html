BLUE ORIGIN LANDINGS — try the four scale options

Same 3 missions in each. The difference is how realistic the booster altitude / speed / entry feel. Click a level to jump straight in (dev gate bypasses unlocks). Press X in-flight for 2× fast-forward — useful for the higher/longer descents in B and C.

Baseline (arcade) — leave as-is

The current compressed arcade game. Separation ~12 km, low speeds. Tight and legible.

7×2 9×4 MK1 menu

A — Arcade + realistic HUD

Identical gameplay to baseline, but the telemetry reads realistically (×6 display scale, plus a Mach number). Physics untouched. Lowest-risk way to stop it feeling obviously wrong.

7×2 9×4 MK1

B — Moderate scale-up

Believable separation regime (~26–32 km, ~Mach 1.5–2), air starts at ~24 km so the descent is longer and more realistic. Still hand-flyable; use X to fast-forward the coast.

7×2 9×4 MK1

C — Full real-scale entry

Real reentry regime: separation ~65–70 km at ~Mach 5–6, the atmosphere brakes you (peaks ~5 g), then a suicide burn onto Jacklyn. Downrange is still compressed to fit the screen. Hardest, most sim-like.

7×2 9×4 MK1

All four are independent copies. MK1 (the lunar lander) is the same in every variant — the scale changes only affect the New Glenn booster descents (7×2 / 9×4). After you pick a direction, that variant becomes the product and the others can be deleted.
